Dr. Georgios Papanikolaou

The man who has saved and continues to save millions of women in the world through the Pap Test.

The timeline of his life

  • 1883 - Born in Kymi, Greece.
  • 1898 - At the age of 15, Papanikolaou attended the University of Athens Medical School.
  • 1904 - Received his medical degree from the University of Athens.
  • 1906 - After serving in the Armed Forces he returned to Kymi where he practiced medicine with his father.
  • 1907 - Studied at the University of Jena in Germany
  • 1910 - Graduated with a Phd in zoology from the University of Munich. Married his wife Andromachi Mary Papanikolaou in Athens.
  • 1913 - Immigrated to New York with his wife to work in the department of Pathology of New York Hospital and the Department of Anatomy at the Cornell Medical College of Cornell University.
  • 1914 - Papanikolaou and his wife worked at the Department of Anatomy at the Cornell Medical College of Cornell University and contributed to the histological and physiological changes associated with the oestrus cycle in the guinea pig.
  • 1917 - Papanikolaou along with Charles R. Stockard demonstrated that, in the guinea pig, the histologic cyclic changes that occur in the reproductive tract during the estrus cycle also occur in the vaginal mucosa and can be detected by cytologic examination of vaginal smears.This technique (termed the Papanikolaou technique) was groundbreaking and also facilitated the discovery of an ovarian hormone. Papanikolaou later began examining the human reproductive system.
  • 1920 - He realized that he could tell the difference between normal and malignant cells on the cervix by viewing smears on a slide under a microscope.
  • 1925 - With funds from the National Research Council and the Maternal Health Committee, Papanikolaou recruited 12 hospital staff volunteers, together with a number of pregnant gynecological and surgical patients, for a systematic study of cervical cell morphology.
  • 1928 - He first reported that uterine cancer could be diagnosed by means of a vaginal smear, but the importance of his work was not recognized until 1943.
  • 1950 - He received the Albert Lasker Award for Clinical Medical Research (the American equivalent to the Nobel Prize in Physiology). Papanikolaou received many prestigious prizes and awards for his discoveries.
  • 1954 - He published he Atlas of Exfoliative Cytology, thus creating the foundation of the modern medical specialty of Cytopathology.
  • 1961 - He moved to Miami, Florida, to develop the Papanikolaou Cancer Research Institute at the University of Miami.
  • 1962 - Died due to a myocardial infarction at the age of 78. His wife continued his work until her death in 1982.

"The first observation of cancer cells in the smear of the uterine cervix gave me one of the greatest thrills I ever experienced during my scientific career".

- Dr. Georgios Papanikolaou

Other facts

  • The Pap smear test (AE) / cervical smear (BE), remains an effective, widely used method for early detection of precancer and cervical cancer and is used all over the world.
  • By some accounts, the Pap test is considered the most significant advance in the control of cancer in the 20th century.
  • The Pap Smear Test is saving lives! Women owe also to Andromachi Mary Papanikolaou, his wife, who became his laboratory assistant and research subject. For 21 years, Mary Papanikolaou volunteered as an experimental subject for her husband, climbing up onto his examination couch every day so that he could sample and smear her cervix.

"There was no other option but for me to follow him inside the lab, making his way of life mine".

- Andromachi Mary Papanikolaou
